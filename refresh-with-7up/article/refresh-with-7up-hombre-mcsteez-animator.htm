A chat with Marty

How long have you been a cartoonist and how has your work evolved over time?

MARTY: I have drawn cartoons since I could hold a pencil. I worked in the feature animation business for a while, drawing storyboards for animated movies. I began animating on cels for fun and it evolved into a career!

How did you get into creating the augmented reality cartoons?

MARTY: It started from holding a piece of glass up and drawing on it with a dry erase marker. Then I began using Tipp-ex and Sharpies and Instagramming the photos. Eventually I tried to animate one of them and it was really difficult - but the result was unexpectedly awesome looking.

What, who and where do you draw your inspiration from?

MARTY: I am inspired by people who look at life a little differently. People like Mark Gonzales, Andy Goldsworthy, Pablo Picasso and Jim Henson.

Why did you decide to work with 7UP and how have you found the partnership?

MARTY: 7up came to me with an open mind and asked me to come up with a commercial. I gave them some pretty crazy ideas and they were totally on board! It was a fun and relaxed creative process. Shooting the commercial was a little different than my normal process because of the scale, but it was a fun challenge and I got to meet and work with some really creative, awesome people.

What is the key to your originality?

MARTY: I've never watched Star Wars.
